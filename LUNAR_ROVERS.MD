# Lunar Rovers

Lunar rovers are robotic vehicles designed for exploration and surface operations on the Moon. Various organizations worldwide are developing lunar rovers in different stages of readiness, indicating growing interest in lunar exploration.

## Human Scale & LTV Candidates

NASA's **Lunar Terrain Vehicle (LTV)** services contract is being competed for by three primary teams. These unpressurized rovers will support Artemis astronauts.

1.  **Lunar Outpost** - "Lunar Dawn" Team (LTV Name: Eagle) (Partners: Lockheed Martin, MDA Space, GM, Goodyear)
2.  **Venturi Astrolab** - "FLEX" LTV (Team: Axiom Space, Odyssey Space Research)
3.  **Intuitive Machines** - "Moon RACER" (Team: AVL, Boeing, Michelin, Northrop Grumman) 



## Small Lunar Rovers & Micro-Rovers

Small lunar rovers (<10 kg) are a growing field in lunar exploration, often focusing on swarm capabilities and low-cost deployment.

| Rover | Developer | Size / Weight | Key Features |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **CADRE** | NASA JPL | ~Size of carry-on bag | **Autonomous Swarm**. Trio of rovers for cooperative mapping. |
| **Lunar Zebro** | TU Delft | A4 sheet size, 2.5 kg | **Walking Rover**. 6 C-shaped legs for difficult terrain. |
| **Sora-Q (LEV-2)** | JAXA / Tomy | 8 cm diameter, 250 g | **Transformable**. Sphere to rover. Deployed by SLIM (2024). |
| **Iris (CubeRover)** | Carnegie Mellon | Shoebox size, 2 kg | **Standardized**. CubeSat-style rover standards. |
| **Colmena** | UNAM (Mexico) | <1 inch tall, <60 g | **Micro-swarm**. 5 tiny robots studying lunar dust. |
| **Yaoki** | Dymon | 15x15 cm, 0.5 kg | **All-Terrain**. Two-wheeled, durable. |
| **CHERI** | M. East Technical University / Zhejiang University / Star Vision | 5kg, shoebox size | 2 rovers conducting 3D mapping, measure temperature and radiation levels, & share navigational data |
| **Asagumo** | Spacebit (UK) | 1.3 kg, 1U CubeSat size | **Walking Rover**. Spider-like 4-legged micro-rover. Lidar/HD Cam. |
| **MoonRanger** | CMU / NASA | 18 kg (Earth), Suitcase | **Autonomous**. South Pole ice seeker. Fast (1km/day). Precursor to VIPER. |
| **PitRanger** | CMU / Astrobotic | Micro-class | **Pit Explorer**. Autonomous, fast, solar-powered for lunar pits. |
| **TENACIOUS** | ispace-EUROPE (Luxembourg) | 26x31.5x54 cm, 5 kg | **Micro-rover**. CFRP frame, HD camera. Collecting regolith for NASA. Mission 2 (Resilience). |


**Key Characteristics:**
- **Mass:** Typically under 10 kg.
- **Locomotion:** Often 4-wheel skid-steer or novel types (legs, shape-shifting).
- **Autonomy:** Heavy reliance on autonomy due to communication latency and swarm operations.



## Other Small & Research Rovers

These projects represent the cutting edge of university and research institute micro-rover development.

- **Colmena (UNAM):** 5 tiny (60g) autonomous robots for swarm testing.
- **Lunar Zebro (TU Delft):** C-shaped legged rover for difficult terrain.
- **SAMLER-KI (DFKI/FH Aachen):** AI-driven semi-autonomous micro-rover for lunar night survival.
- **Micro Nova Hopper (Intuitive Machines):** Propulsive "hopper" (Grace) for extreme terrain/craters.
