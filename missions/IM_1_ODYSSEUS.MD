# IM-1 (Odysseus)

## Overview
First successful commercial soft landing. The mission delivered six NASA CLPS payloads and six commercial payloads to the lunar south pole region.

## Details
*   **Lander:** [Nova-C](../landers/NOVA_C.MD)
*   **Transfer Trajectory:** Direct (Launch Feb 15, 2024). Transit duration: **7.7 days**.
*   **Connectivity:** 
    *   **Direct-to-Earth:** S-Band link with rates up to 6 Mbps.
    *   **Security:** All payload data downlinked with **AES-256 encryption**.
*   **Payloads:**
    *   **NASA CLPS:**
        *   **LRA** (Laser Retroreflector Array) - NASA GSFC
        *   **NDL** (Navigation Doppler Lidar) - NASA LaRC (Used as backup for landing navigation)
        *   **SCALPSS** (Stereo Cameras for Lunar Plume-Surface Studies) - NASA LaRC
        *   **ROLSES** (Radio Operations on the Lunar Surface) - NASA GSFC
        *   **LN-1** (Lunar Node 1 Navigation Demonstrator) - NASA MSFC
        *   **RFMG** (Radio Frequency Mass Gauge) - NASA GRC
    *   **Commercial/Other:**
        *   **EagleCam** (CubeSat camera system) - [Embry-Riddle Aeronautical Univ.](../rovers/EAGLECAM.MD) (Deployed but data return limited)
        *   **ILO-X** (International Lunar Observatory) - ILOA
        *   **Omni-Heat Infinity** (Thermal coating test) - Columbia Sportswear
        *   **Lunaprise** (Digital Archive) - Galactic Legacy Labs
        *   **Independence** (Data Center test) - Lonestar Data Holdings
        *   **Moon Phases** (Art Installation) - Jeff Koons
*   **Status:** Success / Partial Function (Feb 22, 2024). First commercial soft landing. Landed at Malapert A; the lander tripped on a rock upon touchdown and came to rest on its side, but remained functional for ~7 days until lunar sunset.
